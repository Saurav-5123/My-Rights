Child Protection Laws

Know the laws related to child labor, education rights, and child abuse prevention.

Children are among the most vulnerable sections of society, and the Indian legal system provides several constitutional provisions, statutory laws, and government schemes aimed at their protection. Child Protection Laws in India are designed to safeguard children from exploitation, abuse, neglect, and harmful practices, and to ensure their development, education, and welfare.

These laws address issues such as child labor, child marriage, abuse, trafficking, and educational rights, and they place a strong emphasis on the best interests of the child.

Key Laws for Child Protection in India:

1. The Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act, 2012

  • Comprehensive legal framework for sexual offences against children
  • Child-friendly procedures during investigation and trial
  • Defines child as anyone below 18 years
  • Mandatory reporting for parents, teachers, caregivers

2. The Child Labour (Prohibition and Regulation) Act, 1986 (Amended in 2016)

  • Prohibits employment of children under 14 in any occupation
  • Adolescents (14–18) banned from hazardous jobs
  • Allows work in non-hazardous family businesses with conditions

3. The Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act (RTE), 2009

  • Guarantees education for children aged 6 to 14
  • No discrimination, punishment, or harassment allowed
  • Special focus on children from disadvantaged groups

4. Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015

  • Rehabilitation for children in conflict with law
  • Child Welfare Committees and Juvenile Justice Boards
  • 16–18 year-olds may be tried as adults for heinous crimes

5. The Prohibition of Child Marriage Act, 2006

  • Prohibits marriage for girls below 18 and boys below 21
  • Provides for protection and maintenance of affected children
  • Prescribes penalties for promoting or conducting child marriage

6. National Commission for Protection of Child Rights (NCPCR)

  • Monitors all laws and policies related to children
  • Receives and resolves complaints
  • Advises government on child rights policy matters

Other Important Initiatives:

  • Integrated Child Protection Scheme (ICPS)
  • TrackChild Portal – to track missing/found children
  • POSHAN Abhiyaan – nutrition-focused scheme
  • Childline 1098 – 24x7 emergency helpline

Rights of Children:

  • Right to Survival and Development
  • Right to Protection from Exploitation
  • Right to Education
  • Right to Participate in Decisions
  • Right to Health and Nutrition

Challenges:

  • Underreporting of child abuse
  • Lack of awareness about child rights
  • Poverty and illiteracy fueling exploitation
  • Inadequate rehab and support infrastructure
