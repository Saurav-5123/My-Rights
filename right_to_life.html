Right to Life

Guarantees protection of life and personal liberty.

The Right to Life is a cornerstone of the Indian Constitution and is enshrined in Article 21. It ensures that every individual has the right to live with dignity, and that their life and personal liberty cannot be taken away except through a legal and just procedure.

This right has been widely interpreted by the judiciary to include various aspects essential for a meaningful and dignified life, extending far beyond mere survival.

Article 21 – Protection of Life and Personal Liberty

Text:
“No person shall be deprived of his life or personal liberty except according to procedure established by law.”

This means:
The State cannot take away a person’s life or liberty unless it follows fair, just, and reasonable legal procedures.

The Supreme Court has expanded this article to include various rights implicit in living a dignified life.

Key Rights and Aspects Under the Right to Life:

  • Right to Live with Human Dignity
  • Right to Privacy
  • Right to Clean Environment
  • Right to Health and Medical Care
  • Right to Education
  • Right to Shelter
  • Right Against Police Brutality and Illegal Detention
  • Right to Legal Aid and Fair Trial
  • Right to Die with Dignity

Importance of the Right to Life:

  • Serves as the foundation of all other fundamental rights.
  • Promotes the rule of law and safeguards individual freedom.
  • Helps ensure a civilized and humane society.
  • Empowers citizens to hold the State accountable for violations affecting their life and liberty.

Limitations:

While broad, the Right to Life is not absolute. It can be curtailed:

  • By law, provided the law follows fair, just, and reasonable procedures.
  • In cases of death penalty, preventive detention, or national emergencies, though with strict checks.
